How my time studying for University affects my daily habits

Hypothesis

As the time I spend studying for University increases,the number of habits I do daily decreases.

Overview

In the beginnig, for this project I wanted to evaluate if the time I dedicated to my studies had any influence on the time I dedicated to my daily habits. As the time went by and the project evolved, I decided to slightly modify my question mainly because of the visualization that I wanted to do. This new question was basically the same but instead comparing times, I'm only evaluating if the increase of my worload forces me to not make my daily habits. To achieve this, I decided to collect different types of data on a daily basis that would help me deny or verify my hypothesis. These are the types of data that I initially collected:

  • Date:I collected this data to keep a record of what happened everyday. If my theory ended up being right I would have seen a clear lack of my daily habits as the end of the quarter approached.
  • Time of day:This helped me to keep a better track of every data, because sometimes in a day I would have multiple sessions of study or of any habit.
  • Studying duration:I collected this data in minutes to keep track of the amount of time that I dedicated to my studies
  • Studying music duration:I collected this data in minutes to keep track of the amount of time that I dedicated to studying music, so that later I could see which days I did this habit and which days I did not. Also I thought it would be interesting to have this information to analyze how the time I dedicated to this habit was affected by my workload.
  • Studying french duration:I collected this data in minutes to keep track of the amount of time that I dedicated to studying french, so that later I could see which days I did this habit and which days I did not. Also I thought it would be interesting to have this information to analyze how the time I dedicated to this habit was affected by my workload.
  • Reading duration:I collected this data in minutes to keep track of the amount of time that I dedicated to reading, so that later I could see which days I did this habit and which days I did not. Also I thought it would be interesting to have this information to analyze how the time I dedicated to this habit was affected by my workload.
  • Sleeping duration:I collected this data in minutes to keep track of the amount of time that I dedicated to sleeping, so that later I could see which days I did this habit and which days I did not. Also I thought it would be interesting to have this information to analyze how the time I dedicated to this habit was affected by my workload.
  • Energy levels:I collected this data using a scale from 1 to 5, with 1 being low energy and 5 high energy, because I thought there would also be a correlation between my energy levels and my daily habits

After doing some experimenting with different visualization I noticed that I also needed to include take in my data the days in which I had classes. Then this evolved to consider the different types of day that I could have, my free days during the week days, the days in which I had class and the weekend.

I consider that this data gave me enough information for my visualization. There is only one more piece of data that would have helped me to better understand my day to day and that is the amount of I spend in my cellphone, because that is also another variable that can affect my daily habits. Nevertheless, this visualization gave me a good understanding of what I wanted to evaluate

    • Free Days(Circles):These days show to be my most productive studying sessions, specially some of the ones before days I have class. It is interesting to see that the majority of those days have a complete or almost complete (3 petals) flower, 13 out of 18
    • Weekends(Squares):These days show a high variability. There are weekends with zero study time where habits also disappear, and weekends with high study time where habits are very consistent.
  • Habit persistence:The "Sleeping" and "French" habits appear to be the most persistent across all types of days, while "Music" and "Reading" are more easily affected by my schedule

Conclusion

In conclusion, the data demonstrates that my daily habits do not depend exclusively on my academic workload. Although I initially expected that more study time would lead to fewer habits, the visualization reveals that I am able to maintain my priorities even on high-demand days. This suggests that I have developed a routine structure that resists university stress, prioritizing rest over other activities like reading.
