How my time studying for University affects my daily habits

Hypothesis:As the time I spend studying for University increases, the number of habits I do daily decreases.

Work Explanation

The visualization analyzes if there is any correlation between the time I dedicate to my studies and my my daily habits. I wanted to confirm if my studies have any influence in the frequency of my daily habits. As you can see in the visualization, I have the minutes dedicated to my studies, the dates I collected my data and my daily habits. Each day is represented by a flower, its height represents the amount of time dedicated to my studies and each petal represents a habit, that is why each petal has a different color. If a flower doesn't have a petal, it means that I didn't do that habit that day and if there is not stem, it means that I didn't study that day

Observations

  • As it can be observed, there isn't a clear pattern that fully supports the hypothesis, considering that some days in which I had little amount of study, I didn't perform all of my daily habits.
  • There is a range in which I seem to have managed to do almost all my daily habits while still studying, this range is between the 100 and 275 minutes.
  • The visualization also shows with which daily habit I was most persistent, this one was sleeping, demostrating that I did not sacrificed my sleep to get any of my assignments done. The second one was studying french, the thrid one was studying music and the fourth and most affected was reading.

Conclusion

In conclusion, the data demonstrates that my daily habits do not depend exclusively on my academic workload. Although I initially expected that more study time would lead to fewer habits, the visualization reveals that I am able to maintain my priorities even on high-demand days. This suggests that I have developed a routine structure that resists university stress, prioritizing rest over other activities like reading.
