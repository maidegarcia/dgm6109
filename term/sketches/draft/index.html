Phase 3b Term Project Sketches

Sketch 1 - Scatterplot Variant

This first sketch serves as the baseline for my research, utilizing a Scatterplot to analyze the direct correlation between academic workload and personal daily habits. It maps out how much I study (X-Axis) versus how much time I spend doing me daily habits (Y-Axis). By playing with the size of the bubbles, I can also see if my energy levels have any influence on my daily habits.

  • Hypothesis:As the time I spend studying for university increases, the time I spend on my daily habits decreases. Additionally, this is negatively influenced by my energy levels and whether it was a classes day or a free day.
  • Data:
    • Time studying(minutes)
    • Time spent on daily habits(minutes)
    • Energy level (1-5)
    • Classes day (Yes or No)

Sketch 2 - Lollipop Chart

For this second one, I wanted to see the how my daily workload affected my sleeping habit. By using a Lollipop Chart, I can track each day and see if there is any correlation between my daily studying sessions (represented by the color of the lines, each color represents a different time range) and my sleep duration (Y-Axis). I also included my energy levels (represented by circles attached to the colored lines; the circles have different radius depending on the energy level) to evaluate if they also affect my sleeping habit. I got the idea of using a Lollipop Chart after watching the website From Data to Viz, they show different ways of data visualization. In my opinion the Lollipop Chart was the right choice for me as I wanted to include the dates (X-Axis)to have a clearer idea of the daily fluctuations of my data.

  • Hypothesis:As the time I spend studying for university increases, my sleep duration decreases. Additionally, this is negatively influenced by my energy levels.
  • Data:
    • Time studying(0 min:yellow, 1-120 min:orange, 121-240 min:red, 241-360 min:blue, 360-600:green)
    • Sleeping duration(minutes)
    • Date(dd/mm/yy)
    • Energy level (1-5)

Sketch 3 - Flower Data

In this visualization, I am evaluating how my workload and energy levels affect the consistency of my daily habits. I actually developed this idea as an evolution of the Lollipop Chart; I noticed that a line with a circle at the top resembled a flower without petals. In this visualization, each flower represents a day: the length of the stem represents the study duration, the number of leaves indicates my energy level, and each petal represents a specific habit, with its color indicating the category. This serves as a visual representation of how well I managed my routine even when the workload was significant or my energy was low; it also shows which habit was the one got to do the most. To add an interactive element, hovering over a petal will display the exact duration of that habit in minutes.

  • Hypothesis:As the time I spend studying for university increases, the number of my daily habits decreases. Additionally, this reduction in habits can also be affected negatively by my energy levels.
  • Data:
    • Time studying (minutes)
    • Daily habits (practicing music:orange, studying french:pink,reading:blue, sleeping:yellow)
    • Energy level (1-5)
    • Date (dd/mm/yy)
